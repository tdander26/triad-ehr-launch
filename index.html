The problem

You're paying insurance prices for software that doesn't speak AK.

Let's be honest about your current setup.

You run a cash practice. You haven't filed an insurance claim in years. But you're still paying $159, $199, even $306 a month for an EHR built around a clearinghouse and a claim-scrubber you never touch. You're renting billing machinery for a practice that doesn't bill insurance.

And the charting? It's worse.

You were trained at ICAK. You think in challenges, muscle tests, valves, organ indicators, cranial faults, offenders, reflexes. But your EHR has never heard of any of it. So you cram every finding into a free-text SOAP box you hate, hopping between tabs, scrolling for fields, fighting software that was clearly built for somebody else. By the time you've documented one visit the way you actually practice, the next patient is already waiting.

Then there's the picture. A patient asks, "Am I actually getting better?" And all you've got is "trust me." No way to show them the progression across visits. No way to point at the screen and say, look, here's your case turning from red to green.

You didn't get into AK to fight a keyboard. You got into it for what happens at the table.

That's the whole reason Triad exists.
